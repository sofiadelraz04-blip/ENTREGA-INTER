Project 001

Cúo

Cúo started with one main objective: creating a paper lamp.

During my research, I found a technique called "processed paper" and fell in love. Inspired by the work of Pia Wistenberg, I had to figure out the steps for this technique, since there was little to no information on how to make it.

My concept was the end. The processed paper technique shows a lot of layers, which I interpreted as everything that creates a life, ending in one last heartbeat, which I wanted to translate into the shape.

For the sketches, I mainly focused on the rhythm and the organicity.

With recycled books and magazines —which I found poetic, since life is a set of stories—, I started doing paper rolls until they had enough thickness to be machined on the lathe.

After trial and error,I finally got the final pieces to build the lamp.
